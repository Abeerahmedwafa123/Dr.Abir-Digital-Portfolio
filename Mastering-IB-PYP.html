Introduction to IB & PYP Foundations

  • ✔ PYP vs. MYP (understanding the continuum clearly)
  • ✔ The IB Learner Profile (beyond memorization → real application)
  • ✔ Transdisciplinary themes made simple and practical
  • ✔ Building Essential Agreements for your classroom

You’ll develop a clear IB mindset and understand how it shapes teaching, learning, and classroom culture.

Designing Inquiry the IB Way

📌 The heart of PYP

You will master:
  • ✔ Writing powerful Central Ideas
  • ✔ Creating meaningful Lines of Inquiry
  • ✔ Using Key Concepts with purpose (not as labels)
  • ✔ Embedding Approaches to Learning (ATLs) naturally
🎯 Outcome

You’ll confidently design clear, purposeful, inquiry-driven units.

From Philosophy to Practice (Lesson Design)

📌 Turning ideas into real teaching

You will learn:
  • ✔ How to translate IB philosophy into daily lessons
  • ✔ Structuring lessons using: Inquiry stages, Guided discovery, Student-centered strategies
  • ✔ Aligning: Objectives, Activities, Assessment
🎯 Outcome

You’ll be able to design engaging IB-style lessons that actually work in real classrooms.

Split-Screen Teaching Strategy

📌 Teaching with depth and purpose

You will explore:
  • ✔ The 3 dimensions of powerful teaching:
    • The What → What students are learning • The Why → Why it matters • The Who → Who students are becoming
  • ✔ Connecting learning across the IB continuum
  • ✔ Designing lessons that build identity, purpose, and understanding
🎯 Outcome

You’ll shift from content delivery to meaningful, purpose-driven teaching.

Designing Powerful Questions & Deep Thinking

📌 Where real learning happens

You will master:
  • ✔ SOLO Taxonomy (Surface → Deep → Extended thinking)
  • ✔ Moving from simple questioning to intentional thinking design
  • ✔ Using the Question Grid Strategy to: Generate high-quality questions instantly, Differentiate questioning levels, Promote inquiry and student discussion
💡 The Power Combo:

SOLO → defines depth of thinking
Question Grid → generates powerful questions

🎯 Outcome

You’ll design questions that drive curiosity, critical thinking, and deep learning.
